01
Initial (Initial Level)
Situational & Reactive

Core Essence: First steps from chaos to order

Characteristics: Spontaneity, chaotic operational behaviors, "firefighting", informal processes, short-term vision

Focus: Daily survival, immediate response to problems

02
Managed (Managed Level)
Disciplined

Core Essence: First structures and initial signs of order

Characteristics: Documentation of core processes, similar tasks performed with similar methods, basic controls

Focus: Achieving stability, control mechanisms, streamlining core operations

03
Defined (Defined Level)
Standardized & Proactive

Core Essence: Formal systems and proactive planning

Characteristics: Stable operating system, documented and standardized processes, proactive approach

Focus: Process control, transparency and clarity of functions

04
Quantitative (Quantitatively Managed)
Predictable & Measurable Results

Core Essence: Well-functioning process system with clear ownership and measurable results

Characteristics: Continuous monitoring of results (KPIs), data-driven decisions, statistical control

Focus: Achieving strategic goals, measuring and forecasting efficiency, optimization

05
Optimized (Optimized Level)
Continuous improvement & rapid adaptation

Core Essence: Constant perfection and shaping the future

Characteristics: Continuous improvement, innovation culture, high adaptability, automatic detection of weak points

Focus: Innovation, learning, adaptation, market leadership, sustainable development

IMPORTANCE

Why Is Understanding Process Maturity Important?

Knowing the maturity level of your organization's business processes gives you several important advantages:

Self-Awareness
Helps you assess your current state, strengths and weaknesses. Determine whether your business depends on individual knowledge and "heroics" or is built on well-established systems.
Quality Control
Helps you understand why service quality is inconsistent.
Stability
Measuring and monitoring business process results enables you to make data-driven decisions, which is a prerequisite for a predictable and successful business.
Internal Communication
Creates a shared language and vision within the organization for managing and improving business processes. Everyone knows who does what and how.
Sustainable Success
Aligning well-functioning business processes with strategic goals is directly linked to the organization's long-term success and competitiveness.
Growth
Gives you a clear picture of the steps needed to transition to the next stage of development.
ASSESSMENT

Your Organization's Business Process Maturity Assessment: Phase 1

What Does Phase 1 Mean: Initial — Situational & Reactive

The questionnaire results show that your organization is currently at the first phase of business process maturity, i.e., the initial stage of development (situational and reactive), which is natural for an organization in the early stages of growth. This is the stage where the foundation begins to be laid for a more structured and predictable future.

At this stage, your organization is likely characterized by spontaneous and reactive actions. This means that:

This report will help you better understand:

  • 1 What this phase means for your organization.
  • 2 What the key characteristics of this stage are.
  • 3 What challenges your organization may be facing.
  • 4 What steps you can take toward further development.

Key Characteristics

Daily "Firefighting"
Work is completed based on emerging needs, i.e., reactively.
Absence of Operational Culture
There is no established operational culture. Processes are undocumented, informal, and inconsistent.
Stability Issues
Lack of standardization and chaotic operational behaviors lead to inconsistent results and low quality.
Focus on Short-Term Results
The main concern is maintaining current operations and solving immediate problems.
Success Depends on Specific Individuals
You may have "heroes" whose knowledge and experience drive success, while a systematic approach is underdeveloped.
Experience and Knowledge Are Hard to Retain
An employee's knowledge and experience are their personal asset, not the organization's. If they leave, they take their knowledge with them. There is no knowledge transfer.
Lack of Proper Communication
There is no shared understanding of how work is carried out. Employees do not feel engaged and do not clearly understand what strategic goals the organization's activities serve.
Broken Bridges Between Departments
Departments work in isolation, functional gaps are frequent. There is no single person responsible for the entire process ("process owner"). When problems arise, blame is shifted between departments.
Frequent Operational Failures
There is no root cause analysis of problems. As a result, problems recur.
MANIFESTATION

How Does This Manifest in Your Organization's Operations?

Your questionnaire responses indicate that the organization acts spontaneously and reacts to events rather than proactively planning for the future.

CHALLENGES

What Are the Typical Challenges at This Phase?

Unclear Direction
It is difficult to understand where the organization is heading in the long term.
Unstable Results
Sometimes results are good, sometimes not. It is difficult to determine the causes.
Exhausted Employees
The constant "emergency" mode and heavy workload on specific individuals can lead to employee burnout.
Growth Impediment
Business expansion is impossible under chaotic conditions.
Delayed Response to Change
It is difficult to adapt quickly to market changes when you are constantly occupied with ongoing problems.
Inefficient Use of Resources
Human and non-human resources (time, money) may be spent on less important matters.
CONCLUSION

Conclusion

Your organization is currently in the "Initial" phase of business process development. To move to the next stage and continue growing, it is necessary to properly set strategic goals, develop strategy, define and document core processes. Additionally, proper communication within the organization must be established so that every employee feels engaged and understands the goals the organization's activities serve.

What Positive Results Will Moving to the Next Stage Bring?

Predictable Results
Quality is guaranteed, not random; fewer errors.
Easy Adaptation
New employees quickly get up to speed.
Less Stress
More time for strategic goals, less time "firefighting".
Growth Without Chaos
Systems that can handle increasing workloads.
